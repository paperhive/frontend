Privacy Policy

Your privacy is important to us, and it is PaperHive's policy to respect your privacy regarding any information we may collect while operating our websites and services. Accordingly, we have developed this policy in order for you to understand how we collect, use, communicate,disclose, and make use of personal information. The following outlines our privacy policy.

Guiding Principles

These are our guiding principles when collecting, storing and processing your personal data:

  • Before or at the time of collecting personal information, we will identify the purposes for which information is being collected.
  • We will collect and make use of personal information solely with the objective of fulfilling those purposes specified by us and for other compatible purposes, unless we obtain the consent of the individual concerned or as required by law.
  • We will only retain personal information as long as necessary for the fulfillment of those purposes.
  • We will collect personal information by lawful and fair means and, where appropriate, with the knowledge or consent of the individual concerned.
  • Personal data should be relevant to the purposes for which it is to be used, and, to the extent necessary for those purposes, should be accurate, complete, and up-to-date.
  • We will protect personal information by reasonable security safeguards against loss or theft, as well as unauthorized access, disclosure, copying, use or modification.
  • We will make readily available to users information about our policies and practices relating to the management of personal information.

What information we collect and how we use it

Account Information

In order to use PaperHive’s services, e.g., to annotate publicly available or privately uploaded documents you need to create an account. The personal data we collect for this includes a username, a display name, and depending on how you create the account an email and a password, or identifiers that allow you to log in via third parties (e.g., with Google, ORCID, or other third-party services).

Contact Information

We use your email address to:

  • authenticate you,
  • verify your account,
  • send you notifications for other users’ public or private activity on documents you are interested in,
  • send you updates about our services.
You may opt out of receiving promotional email messages (e.g., by using the unsubscribe link with each email). You will still be able to receive messages regarding your account and the activity around the documents you are interested in. You can regulate the frequency of some notification messages in your account settings.

Public Information You Share or Generate

The only publicly visible information you share is:

  • Your public contributions including the title, the text body, the time of creation, and the part of the document that is referenced
  • Your subscriptions to publicly available documents,
  • Your username and your display name which can also be pseudonyms.
You agree to license your public contributions (public discussions, including replies and all embedded material such as formulas, images, videos) under the CC-BY-4.0 license, see also the CC-BY-4.0 overview.

You are responsible for your public contributions and you should think carefully about which of your comments should be made public and which private.

Public contributions are also exposed via our application programming interface (API) for use by other individuals and organizations. PaperHive’s widget can be embedded in third-party websites to display the public activity on a publication. PaperHive is not necessarily affiliated with entities using or displaying the publicly visibilily activity.

Private Information You Share or Generate

Document uploads: PaperHive allows you to upload documents privately. We store and process uploaded documents. This includes extracting metadata, matching with published documents, indexing for making them searchable, and aggregating metadata for generating anonymized statistics. By default your uploaded documents are not shared with other users and only you can grant others access to your uploaded documents. We do not share the content of private document uploads with anyone or use it for other purposes without your consent. Note that if you share uploaded documents with others they can create a copy.

Private Channels: PaperHive’s Private Channels enable you to share privately uploaded documents and communicate privately with a closed group of users. All discussions and shared documents in a particular Channel will only be visible to the members of this Channel. Members of the Channel need to explicitly be invited by a Channel owner to join it – either by email or a Channel invitation link. Once a new user joins a Channel she will be able to see all documents shared in the Channel as well as read and participate in all previous or new discussions in the Channel.

“Only me” discussions: when creating a discussion you can make it an “only me” discussion that is only visible to yourself.

We store and process private discussions (those in Private Channels and “only me” discussions). This includes indexing for making them searchable and aggregating metadata for generating anonymized statistics. We do not share the content (title and text body) of these discussions with anyone or use it for other purposes without your consent.

Payment Information

In order to access some premium features, you will need to provide us with payment information (e.g., credit card information and billing address). We store and process your payment information only with your consent and only as long as required (e.g., for recurring payments).

Automatically Collected Information

Some information is collected automatically when you visit PaperHive and interact with its features.

Log Data

We automatically receive information, Log Data, about your interactions with our platform, e.g., when you visit the site, sign in, click on links, upload documents, or create new discussions and Channels. This Log Data includes information such as your browser type, operating system, the referring web page, location, IP address, device information, search terms, and cookie information. We anonymize personal identifiers such as IP addresses at the earliest technically possible stage and use them for deriving an approximate location. The full IP address is never written to disk. Note that this information is also collected if you have not created an account.

We use Log Data to improve our platform, protect your data, detect problems or irregularities, conduct analytics, and operate the service reliably. We keep Log Data for a maximum of 24 months.

Cookies and Local Storage

We use cookies and Local Storage to improve your experience on PaperHive. Both cookies and Local Storage are browser technologies that allow us to store small pieces of data in your browser and thus

  • keep you signed in to PaperHive without re-entering your username or password,
  • personalize PaperHive for you,
  • better understand how you interact with PaperHive,
  • monitor aggregated usage patterns.
The cookies only store your personal data in pseudonymous form. You can prevent the use of cookies by changing the settings of your browser to not accept new cookies, to indicate new cookies, and/or to erase all previously stored cookies.

Content on Third-Party Websites

When you view third-party websites that have integrated the PaperHive widget, we may receive Log Data that includes the web page you visited. To prevent the aggregation of such data to a browsing history we delete identifiers such as cookies in addition to measures we apply to all Log Data (such as anonymizing IP addresses). The purpose of collecting this data is to better understand the use of our services and to protect the integrity of our platform.

Whom Do We Share Information with

Service Provider

Third-party companies provide us with services including hosting, storage, backup, maintenance, virtual infrastructure, payment processing, and analytics. Some of these service providers may need to access or use personal information to perform services on your behalf. This always happens under instruction from us and in compliance with policies and procedures designed to protect your data.

Links to Third-Party Sites

PaperHive may include links to other websites whose privacy policies may differ from ours. If you use these websites, the data you share with them will be governed by their privacy policies, not this one.

Enforcement Requests and Applicable Laws

In exceptional situations we may share your information with a third party if we need to:

  • comply with any applicable law, regulation, or legal process
  • protect the security or integrity of our product
  • protect our users or the public from harmful or illegal activities (e.g. to prevent spam and abuse)

Change of Company Ownership

In connection with any merger, acquisition, sale of company assets, or investment, we may share information under this privacy policy with another organization. You will be informed in the case of such a transaction and educated about the choices you have regarding your data.

Managing Your Personal Information with Us

There are several ways you control the personal data you share with us. On PaperHive, you can at any time:

  • access the information,
  • rectify the information,
  • download the information,
  • delete your account.
If you delete your PaperHive account, all your information is deleted except for the annotations made in public. They are shared publicly under the CC-BY-4.0 license and will still be visible.

Children and PaperHive

Children under the age of 13 are not allowed to use PaperHive and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them. If we become aware that a child under 13 has provided us with personal information, we will delete such information. If a parent or guardian becomes aware that his or her child has provided us with personal information without their consent, he or she should contact us at info@paperhive.org.
